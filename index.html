Our bakery

Flour, time,
and obsession.

We have never used a bread improver. We never will.

How we work

The craft
behind
every loaf

01
Mill & Source

Heritage grains milled within 50 miles, ordered weekly for freshness. Single-farm cultured butters.

02
Long Ferment

All doughs cold-ferment 24–72 hours. No shortcuts, no commercial yeast additives. Ever.

03
Hand Shape

Every single loaf shaped by hand. We refuse to let a machine decide the character of your bread.

04
Stone Bake

Deck ovens at 520°F with live steam injection — the only way to achieve the crust we lose sleep over.

Our
Story

Maison Dorée was born in a tiny rented kitchen when Élise Beaumont returned from five years training in Lyon and Paris with one simple conviction: good bread cannot be rushed.

Every loaf here begins 48 hours before it reaches your hands — with a living sourdough starter that has been fed daily since the very first week we opened. We use heritage wheat varieties milled locally, cultured butters from a single farm, and whatever the season brings.

Élise Beaumont
Head Baker & Founder

"We have never used a bread improver. We never will."

— Élise Beaumont
Our journey

Fifteen years of slow bread

2009

The Beginning

Élise returns from Paris and opens Maison Dorée in a 400sqft rented kitchen. 12 loaves a day. Sold out by 9 AM.

2012

First Farmer's Market Award

Best Artisan Bread at the Portland Farmers Market. The queue the following Saturday stretched around the block.

2015

Permanent Home

Move to 14 Rue de la Paix in the Old Town Quarter. First proper deck oven. Croissants added to the menu.

2018

Pâtisserie Launch

Claire Fontaine joins as Head Pâtissier. The tart menu and celebration cake programme begin. Three-month waitlist for custom cakes within weeks.

2024

Bread Subscription Launch

Weekly subscription boxes launched for our most loyal customers. Sold out the first allocation in 47 minutes.

The process

Craft in every step

Good bread is mostly waiting. We do the hard parts so time can do its work — then we get out of the way and let the dough become what it wants to be.

01

Source

Heritage grains, local mills, single-farm butter. No compromises on ingredients.

02

Ferment

24–72 hour cold fermentation. No commercial yeast. Patience is an ingredient.

03

Shape

Every loaf shaped by hand. Machines don't understand what a dough wants to be.

04

Bake

520°F deck ovens with live steam. The crust we dream about every single night.
