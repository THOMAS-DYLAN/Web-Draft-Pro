Our Story

Fifteen years of flour, patience, and an obsessive refusal to compromise on anything that touches the bread.

The beginning

Flour, patience, and fifteen years of practice.

Maison Dorée began in 2009 in a tiny rented kitchen, when Élise Beaumont returned from five years training in Lyon and Paris with one simple conviction: good bread cannot be rushed.

Every loaf here begins 48 hours before it reaches your hands — on a living sourdough starter, named Marguerite, that has been fed every single day since the first week we opened. We use heritage wheat milled within 50 miles, cultured butters from a single farm, and whatever the season brings.

We have never used a bread improver. We never will.

Élise BeaumontHead Baker & Founder

"The oven doesn't lie. What you put in is what you get back."

— Élise Beaumont
Our journey

Fifteen years of slow bread

2009

The Beginning

Élise opens Maison Dorée in a 400sqft rented kitchen. 12 loaves a day. Sold out by 9 AM every morning within the first week.

2012

First Farmers Market Award

Best Artisan Bread at the Portland Farmers Market. The queue the following Saturday stretched around the block for the first time.

2015

Permanent Home

Move to 14 Rue de la Paix in the Old Town Quarter. First proper stone deck oven. Croissants added to the morning menu.

2018

Pâtisserie Launch

Claire Fontaine joins as Head Pâtissier. The full cake and tart programme begins — three-month waitlist for custom cakes within weeks.

2024

Subscription Launch

Weekly bread subscription boxes launch for our most loyal customers. First allocation sold out in 47 minutes.
